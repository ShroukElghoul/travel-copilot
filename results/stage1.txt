STAGE 1 — VECTOR SEARCH ONLY (cosine similarity over embeddings)
================================================================

Q1:
"What is there to see in Aachen?"
A1:
I don't have enough information about Aachen.

Sources:
  - 88 Temple Pilgrimage (Get in)
  - Aalst (Do)
  - Aarhus (Cope)
  - Abbottabad (Go next)
  - Abadan (Go next)

--- ANALYSIS ---
Outcome: FAILED. Not one Aachen chunk retrieved, even though Aachen IS indexed.
Why: The meaningful token is the proper noun "Aachen". Embeddings encode the
  general SENTENCE meaning ("what is there to see in a place"), not the specific
  name, so the model matched the shape of the question and pulled unrelated
  cities' "Do/Go next" sections.
Recall vs precision: RECALL failure — the right content never entered the results.
Good behavior to note: the LLM correctly REFUSED to guess ("I don't have enough
  information") instead of hallucinating — the system prompt's "answer only from
  context" rule working as intended.
Fix: hybrid search (BM25 catches the literal word "Aachen").

CONCEPTUAL QUESTION (the kind vector search handles WELL)

Q3:
"where can I walk around a historic medieval city center?"
A3:
You can walk around a historic medieval city center in 's-Hertogenbosch (Den Bosch),
specifically in its well-preserved medieval centre, as described in [Source 5: 's-Hertogenbosch
(Introduction)]. Additionally, Aarhus also has a historic city center with many
historic buildings and preserved environments that can be explored on foot, such as
the Latin Quarter in [Source 2: Aarhus (See)].
In 's-Hertogenbosch, you can walk along the winding streets to see Saint John's
Cathedral and visit the market square. You can also explore the old fortifications
by walking northwards via certain streets and bastions, as described in [Source 4: 's-Hertogenbosch (See)].

Sources:
  - 's-Hertogenbosch (Get around)
  - Aarhus (See)
  - Aarhus (Understand)
  - 's-Hertogenbosch (See)
  - 's-Hertogenbosch (Introduction)

--- ANALYSIS ---
Outcome: SUCCESS. Correct chunks retrieved, grounded answer, inline citations.
Why: This is a CONCEPTUAL query — no rare proper noun, just meaning ("walkable
  historic medieval centre"). This is exactly the strength of dense/embedding
  search: matching paraphrased meaning, not exact words. The answer drew real
  details from the chunks (Saint John's Cathedral, market square, fortifications).
Recall vs precision: both good here — relevant chunks present and sensibly ordered.

--- STAGE 1 TAKEAWAY (for demo) ---
Vector-only search is strong on CONCEPTS (Q3) but weak on SPECIFIC KEYWORDS / PROPER
NOUNS (Q1, Q2). The failures are RECALL failures: the right content never surfaces.
This is the precise motivation for adding sparse/keyword retrieval (BM25) → Stage 2.
