--- ANALYSIS ---
The arc across all three stages for this query:
1. Vector-only (Stage 1): wrong cities AND often wrong topic. "Aarhus" lost
   entirely — a RECALL failure, right content never showed up.
2. Hybrid (now): right TOPIC and the right city PRESENT (Aarhus at #4), but wrong

   so ordering is off. A PRECISION problem now, not recall.

   and understands "Aarhus trains" beats "Aarau buses" — pushing the right city to
   the top. Fixes precision.
One line: vector-only missed it (recall) → hybrid found it but mis-ordered
  (precision) → re-ranker orders it right. Each stage fixes the previous one's
  weakness.
Note: partly a small-corpus artifact — 20 early-alphabet "Aa..." cities with
  identical section structure is unusually adversarial for keyword matching;
  this noise spreads out on the full corpus.

--- STAGE 2a TAKEAWAY (for demo) ---
Hybrid (BM25 + vector via RRF) fixes the RECALL failures of Stage 1 — proper-noun
queries (Aachen, Aarhus) now surface the right content — WITHOUT degrading the
conceptual queries vector search already did well. What remains is a PRECISION gap:
right candidates present, ordering imperfect. That is the job of the next stage,
cross-encoder RE-RANKING (Stage 2b / FlashRank).
