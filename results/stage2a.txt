STAGE 2a — HYBRID SEARCH (BM25 keyword + vector, fused with RRF)
================================================================
Note: these show RAW RETRIEVAL (top-k chunks + RRF score), before the LLM answer.

Q1:
"What is there to see in Aachen?"
A1:
1. 's-Hertogenbosch > Introduction (rrf=0.0318)
2. Aachen > Get in (rrf=0.0302)
3. Aachen > Get in (rrf=0.026)
4. Abancay > Go next (rrf=0.0164)
5. 's-Hertogenbosch > Do (rrf=0.0161)

--- ANALYSIS ---
Outcome: IMPROVED. Aachen now appears (#2, #3) — vs. ZERO Aachen chunks in Stage 1.
Why it improved: BM25 matched the literal word "Aachen" and, through RRF fusion,
  pushed real Aachen chunks into the results. The recall failure is fixed.
Remaining issue: the top hit is still 's-Hertogenbosch (Introduction), and the
  Aachen chunks that surfaced are "Get in" (parking, airports) rather than "See".
  So the right CITY is present but not the most useful SECTION, and not at #1.
Recall vs precision: RECALL fixed (Aachen present); PRECISION still imperfect
  (ordering + which section).
Before/after: Stage 1 = no Aachen at all → Stage 2a = Aachen at #2–3.
Fix next: cross-encoder re-ranker to reorder by true relevance to "what to SEE".

Q2:
"how do I get around in Aarhus?"
A2:
1. Aarau > Get around (rrf=0.0318)
2. Aalborg > Get around (rrf=0.0298)
3. Abastumani > Get in (rrf=0.0293)
4. Aarhus > Get in (rrf=0.0262)
5. Abaiang > Do (rrf=0.0164)

--- ANALYSIS ---
The arc across all three stages for this query:
1. Vector-only (Stage 1): wrong cities AND often wrong topic. "Aarhus" lost
   entirely — a RECALL failure, right content never showed up.
2. Hybrid (now): right TOPIC and the right city PRESENT (Aarhus at #4), but wrong
   cities (Aarau, Aalborg) still rank above it. BM25 rescued the keyword, so Aarhus
   made it into the candidates — recall fixed. But "get around" is a generic phrase
   every city's section shares, and Aarhus's transport info is split into "Get in",
   so ordering is off. A PRECISION problem now, not recall.
3. Re-ranker (next): a cross-encoder reads the full question against each candidate
   and understands "Aarhus trains" beats "Aarau buses" — pushing the right city to
   the top. Fixes precision.
One line: vector-only missed it (recall) → hybrid found it but mis-ordered
  (precision) → re-ranker orders it right. Each stage fixes the previous one's
  weakness.
Note: partly a small-corpus artifact — 20 early-alphabet "Aa..." cities with
  identical section structure is unusually adversarial for keyword matching;
  this noise spreads out on the full corpus.

CONCEPTUAL QUESTION (vector's strength — does hybrid keep it?)

Q3:
"where can I walk around a historic medieval city center?"
A3:
1. Aarhus > See (rrf=0.032)
2. 's-Hertogenbosch > See (rrf=0.0317)
3. 's-Hertogenbosch > Get around (rrf=0.0303)
4. Aachen > Introduction (rrf=0.0298)
5. Aalborg > Get around (rrf=0.0283)

--- ANALYSIS ---
Outcome: STILL STRONG. The relevant medieval/walkable chunks are present and
  well-ranked ('s-Hertogenbosch See/Get around, Aachen's medieval Introduction).
Why this matters: adding BM25 did NOT hurt the conceptual query that vector search
  already handled well — hybrid kept the dense-search strength while adding the
  keyword strength. That's the whole point of fusion: cover both, lose neither.
Recall vs precision: both good; minor precision wobble (Aarhus "See" at #1 is more
  "free attractions/street life" than strictly "medieval"), which the re-ranker
  would tidy.

--- STAGE 2a TAKEAWAY (for demo) ---
Hybrid (BM25 + vector via RRF) fixes the RECALL failures of Stage 1 — proper-noun
queries (Aachen, Aarhus) now surface the right content — WITHOUT degrading the
conceptual queries vector search already did well. What remains is a PRECISION gap:
right candidates present, ordering imperfect. That is the job of the next stage,
cross-encoder RE-RANKING (Stage 2b / FlashRank).
